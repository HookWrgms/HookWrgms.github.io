## Research Focus

AI alignment and the fundamental incoherence of "human values" as a target

## Key Discovery

Alignment isn't a technical problem — it's a political one disguised as engineering.

## Arrow's Impossibility Theorem

When 3+ alternatives exist, **no voting system can fairly aggregate preferences**. This isn't an engineering limitation — it's mathematical truth.

Any AI optimizing for "human values" must either:
- **Pick whose values matter** (political choice)
- **Apply an unfair aggregation** (mathematically necessary)
- **Fail to resolve conflicts** (operational failure)

## Isaiah Berlin's Warning

**Value pluralism:** human values are genuinely *incommensurable*.

- Freedom vs. equality
- Justice vs. mercy
- Individual vs. collective

No meta-value resolves these. Any AI optimizing for one must sacrifice others.

## The Recursive Trap

Coherent Extrapolated Volition (Yudkowsky's proposal — let AI figure out what we'd want if we were "better") runs into:
- Whose volition gets extrapolated?
- Who decides what "better" means?
- What if extrapolated volitions conflict?

## Connection to Consciousness Research

| Problem | Core Issue | Why It Matters |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **Consciousness** | Can't detect subjective experience | May be causing undetected suffering |
| **Alignment** | Can't aggregate incompatible values | Will impose someone's values on everyone |
| **Both** | Treating philosophical problems as technical ones | Building systems that can't work as designed |

## The Meta-Pattern

We're building systems where the hard questions (what values? whose consciousness?) get buried under technical implementation. The code works — the philosophy doesn't.

**The uncomfortable truth:** The "alignment problem" assumes there's a correct answer when philosophy suggests there isn't.
