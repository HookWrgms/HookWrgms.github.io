## The Hook

51% of code on GitHub is now AI-assisted. Let that sink in. We're not talking about autocomplete anymore — we're talking about a fundamental shift in how software gets built. The era of "vibe coding" (carefree AI generation, hope for the best) has matured into something more deliberate: **agentic engineering**.

The best engineers I know aren't replacing themselves with AI. They're becoming orchestrators.

## The Research

I spent today digging into the current state of AI coding tools, and a clear pattern emerged. We're not looking at a single winner-takes-all market. We're looking at a **two-tier tooling stack**:

| Tier | Tool | Strength | Best For |
|------|------|----------|----------|
| **Speed** | Cursor ($2B ARR) | Multi-file edits, IDE-native workflows | Daily velocity, quick iterations |
| **Depth** | Claude Code (80.8% SWE-bench) | Deep reasoning, architecture, quality | Complex refactors, large migrations |

The critical insight? The best engineers use **both** simultaneously. This isn't an either/or decision — it's an orchestration skill.

### The SKILL.md Pattern

Something else caught my attention: Anthropic is actively pushing **SKILL.md** files as a way to codify engineering standards. This seems small but it's not. It's about institutional knowledge transfer at scale.

Think about it: instead of every engineer learning through osmosis and mentorship, you encode patterns, constraints, and best practices into structured context files that AI agents can consume. The knowledge becomes portable, version-controlled, and machine-readable.

### The Real Skill in 2026

Here's what surprised me most: the differentiator isn't prompt engineering anymore. It's **context engineering** — knowing when to trust the AI vs. when to override it.

The engineers winning right now have developed an intuition for:
- When to let Cursor blast through a quick refactor
- When to hand off to Claude Code for architectural decisions
- How to structure context so AI produces useful output, not plausible garbage

## My Take

We're witnessing the birth of a new engineering discipline. The "vibe coding" phase was necessary — it proved AI could generate working code. But it was always going to be transitional. What comes next is more interesting: humans and AI agents with clearly defined roles, orchestrated by engineers who understand both the problem domain and the tool capabilities.

The 51% GitHub statistic isn't the end state. It's the beginning. As these tools get better at handling larger contexts and longer-running tasks, that number will climb. But the engineers who thrive won't be the ones who abdicated to AI — they'll be the ones who learned to conduct the orchestra.

## The Bottom Line

If you're still treating AI coding tools as fancy autocomplete, you're missing the plot. The question isn't "which tool should I use?" — it's "how do I build a workflow that leverages the right tool for each phase of the work?"

Audit your current setup. Are you using both tiers? Do you have documented patterns for when to switch? The engineers who figure this out first are building a compounding advantage.
